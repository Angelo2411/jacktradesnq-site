## Killzone Range Study — GC Futures (10y)

A **killzone** is a fixed time window where institutional order flow tends to concentrate. This study measures how many points GC travels high-to-low inside each window, across 10 years of 1-minute data (2016–2026), filtering out sessions with zero volume.

### Session hours (New York time, DST auto)

| Killzone | Hours (NY) |
|----------|------------|
| Asia     | 18:00 → 00:00 |
| London   | 02:00 → 05:00 |
| NY AM    | 09:30 → 11:00 |
| NY PM    | 14:00 → 16:10 |

*DST auto-adjusts for US summer/winter time.*

### Headline results (vol>0 sessions only)

| Killzone | Sessions | Avg range | Median range |
|----------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Asia     | 2 671 | 13.2 pts | 7.7 pts |
| London   | 2 669 | 10.2 pts | 7.2 pts |
| NY AM    | 2 670 | 12.9 pts | 9.2 pts |
| NY PM    | 2 618 |  7.4 pts | 4.5 pts |

The mean/median gap is wide for Asia and NY AM — both sessions have fat right tails (occasional spike days lift the average well above the median). NY PM is the quietest window by a large margin.

### News lift — muted on Gold

On NQ, a red-folder news event roughly doubles NY AM range. On Gold the effect is much smaller: approximately +5% on NY AM. Gold reacts to macro data, but the mechanism is slower and more diffuse than equity index futures. Don't expect a news session to transform a quiet Gold day into a volatile one the way it does on NQ.

### Regime shift: 2025–2026

The Gold bull run since late 2024 has materially expanded NY AM ranges:

| Year  | NY AM avg | NY AM median |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| 2024  | ~14 pts   | ~10 pts |
| 2025  | 25.2 pts  | — |
| 2026 (YTD) | 59.1 pts | — |

2026 is exceptional. If you are sizing GC trades using historical average ranges as a stop-distance reference, current conditions require wider stops or reduced size.

### Why It Works

Institutional desks execute GC orders across three overlapping liquidity pools: Asian commodity demand, London fix (10:30 ET), and NY open. Each creates a predictable surge in participation. The killzone framework captures where that participation peaks — and the 10-year data confirms the pattern is stable outside the current volatility expansion.

### What This Means for Trading

- **Asia and NY AM** are the two windows worth watching for GC setups. Range is similar but the macro driver differs: Asia = geopolitical/dollar overnight; NY AM = US data + London close.
- **NY PM** range is roughly half of NY AM. Fade-mode only in that window.
- **Use 2025–2026 data as your live reference** for stop sizing, not the full 10-year average — the regime has shifted.

Conditions cross-checked: weekday, news (red folder), and holiday (NYSE bank holiday).
