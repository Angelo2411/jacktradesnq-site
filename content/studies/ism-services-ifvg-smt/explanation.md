This study tests a reversal entry on Nasdaq 100 (NQ) futures triggered by ISM Services (ISM Services) — the services-sector activity index covering roughly 75% of the US economy, released on the third business day of each month at 10:00 ET. After the release sweeps a pre-news level, you wait for an Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG) to form, then enter when price breaks back inside the range, with an optional S&P 500 (ES) SMT Divergence (SMT) filter. Tested across 2016–2026 on 1-minute NQ data.

Sweep the release-bar high/low (10:00 ET 1m candle), wait for an FVG on the rejection, enter on the IFVG break back inside range · SL = sweep ± 1 tick · TP = opposite liquidity (release-bar pivot). ES SMT keeps only trades where ES also reaches its mirror target within the 10:01-12:00 ET sweep window.

### Why It Works

Services PMI prints mid-week into an established opening range, concentrating liquidity at the release bar extremes. Because services dominates US economic output, NQ and ES react in tandem — requiring ES to confirm the mirror-side sweep filters noise from sector-rotation divergence.
