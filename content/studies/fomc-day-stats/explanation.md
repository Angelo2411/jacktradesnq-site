## Article 1 — FOMC Points

FOMC statement releases at 14:00 ET. Move stats below from 2016 to 2026 — 86 events on NQ.

### Year-by-Year — Release Window Range (5-min, 14:00 ET)

How many NQ points the 5-minute window after the FOMC release travels high-to-low, year by year.

**Range 5m** = the high minus the low (in NQ points) of the 14:00–14:05 ET window.

| Year | Count | Avg Range 5m | Max Range 5m |
|------|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 2016 | 6  | 17.8 pts  | 25.8 pts  |
| 2017 | 8  | 16.9 pts  | 28.0 pts  |
| 2018 | 8  | 41.7 pts  | 146.3 pts |
| 2019 | 9  | 38.3 pts  | 84.0 pts  |
| 2020 | 11 | 40.8 pts  | 84.3 pts  |
| 2021 | 8  | 105.1 pts | 197.5 pts |
| 2022 | 8  | 180.8 pts | 281.0 pts |
| 2023 | 8  | 84.0 pts  | 108.0 pts |
| 2024 | 8  | 109.9 pts | 259.0 pts |
| 2025 | 9  | 85.4 pts  | 148.3 pts |
| 2026 | 3  | 61.6 pts  | 79.5 pts  |

The average 5-minute post-release range went from **17.8 pts** in 2016 to **180.8 pts** in 2022 — a 10× expansion. All-years average: **71.6 pts**, median 53.9, max 281.

### 30-min and 60-min windows

Across the full sample (N=86), the post-release range expands to **96 pts avg** at 30 min and **144 pts avg** at 60 min. FOMC volatility sustains well past the statement bar — unlike CPI/NFP which spike and fade in the first 5 minutes.

## Article 2 — FOMC Direction Bias

15-minute directional drift after release (positive = up). All-years average dir15: **-4.4 pts** (no meaningful bias). 2022 strongest down-skew: -73.7 pts avg. 2021 strongest up-skew: +38.5 pts avg. FOMC is **bidirectional** — no static directional edge across years.
