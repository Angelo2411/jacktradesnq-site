This study tests a reversal entry on Nasdaq 100 (NQ) futures triggered by ISM Manufacturing (ISM Mfg) — the monthly factory-sector activity index released on the first business day of each month at 10:00 ET. After the release sweeps a pre-news level, you wait for an Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG) to form, then enter when price breaks back inside the range, with an optional S&P 500 (ES) SMT Divergence (SMT) filter. A reading above 50 signals expansion; below 50 signals contraction. Tested across 2016–2026 on 1-minute NQ data.

Sweep the release-bar high/low (10:00 ET 1m candle), wait for an FVG on the rejection, enter on the IFVG break back inside range · SL = sweep ± 1 tick · TP = opposite liquidity (release-bar pivot). ES SMT keeps only trades where ES also reaches its mirror target within the 10:01-12:00 ET sweep window.

### Why It Works

ISM Manufacturing is a leading indicator with strong market-moving history. Because it prints at 10:00 ET — after the opening range establishes — liquidity pools form cleanly above/below the release bar. NQ and ES are highly correlated, so requiring ES to confirm the mirror-side sweep filters out one-sided drifts that do not get broad participation.
