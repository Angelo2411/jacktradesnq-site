This study tests a reversal entry on Gold (GC) futures triggered by Durable Goods Orders — the monthly report on long-lived goods manufacturing — at 8:30 ET. After the release sweeps a pre-news level, you wait for an Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG) to form, then enter when price breaks back inside the range, with a Silver (SI) SMT Divergence (SMT) confirmation filter. Tested across 10 years of GC data.

## Model

Same engine as the NQ variant (IFVG inversion + opposite-leg sweep filter + "TP1 + BE" variant), executed on GC. Release time is 08:30 ET (Census Bureau, last full week of month); sweep window extends to 10:30 ET, resolve deadline 16:00 ET.

## SMT pair

NQ variants use ES as the SMT confirmation pair. GC variants use SI (Silver) — the closest correlated metals contract.

## Why This Event

Durable Goods orders signal capex trends and business investment expectations. Stronger-than-expected prints often lift yields and the USD, which pressures gold. The SI co-movement check filters out gold-specific moves (geopolitical, currency hedging) not driven by the macro data itself.
