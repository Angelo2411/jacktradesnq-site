Dow futures (YM) have a high manipulation rate: **60.9%** of trading days (1,391 of 2,284 days, 2016–2024) contain a 09:30–10:00 ET sweep-reject pattern. Like NQ and ES, the Dow's opening hour generates frequent false breakouts at key levels.

The distribution follow-through is **50.9%** — a coin flip. The average post-sweep move is **+0.001 points** (zero), with a median of **1.0 point**. The average favorable excursion is **66.7 points**, the largest MFE among all five assets — but this favorable excursion doesn't translate into closing follow-through. Price travels in the "right" direction intra-hour and then reverses, on average.

An important limitation: YM data ends at December 2024. There is no 2025 or 2026 data available, so we cannot assess whether the pattern persists in the current regime. The 2024+ window (effectively just 2024) shows 48.5% follow-through and a negative average move.

The Dow's manipulation is real and frequent. Its distribution follow-through is random. The large MFE is a mirage — it doesn't close.
