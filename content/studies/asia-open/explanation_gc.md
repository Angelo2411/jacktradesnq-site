## New Week Opening Gap — Fill Study (Gold)

A **New Week Opening Gap (NWOG)** is the difference between GC futures' Friday 16:59 ET close and the Sunday 18:00 ET re-open. I counted gaps of **≥2 points** ($2/oz) — smaller gaps are inside normal tick noise on Gold.

### Definition

- Instrument: GC continuous front-month futures
- Period: 2025-01-01 → 2026-05-12
- Threshold: gap size ≥ 2 pts (absolute, $2/oz)
- Total qualifying gaps: **36** (22 up, 14 down)
- Average absolute gap: **22.4 pts**

Gold shows a structural bull bias in NWOGs — 22 up vs 14 down, reflecting the metal's persistent uptrend and tendency to re-price higher into Asian demand on Sunday night.

### Three gap behaviours

Every gap is classified across three outcomes, measured from the Sunday 18:00 ET open through Monday's cash close:

- **Retrace** — price pulls back at least to the Sunday open price. Measured in the Asia session bucket. **100.0%** of all qualifying gaps retraced in Asia. Every GC gap touches the open.
- **Fill same-day** — the gap closes entirely on Monday. **69.4%** of gaps fill same-day (25 of 36).
- **Flip** — among gaps that filled same-day, **48.0%** continued through the Friday close and traded to the other side. Gold fills are essentially a coin flip on whether they reverse — the lowest flip rate of all four assets, consistent with Gold's trend-following character: when a gap fills, it often stops at the close rather than running through.

Gold fills fast — 33.3% fill in the first 30 minutes, and more than half fill by the end of Asia. The fill curve is front-loaded: NY PM actually has the lowest fill rate (33.3%), which is the pattern you'd expect when most fills happen early and the rest struggle through the day.

### By regime (2025 vs 2026)


Gold's fill rate has declined from 2025 (75.0%) to 2026 (62.5%), and the flip rate dropped from 53.3% to 40.0%. The 2026 sample includes several large January–February gaps that held through the week, pulling the same-day fill number down.

### Lifecycle of non-same-day fills

11 gaps (30.6%) did not fill on Monday. Where did they go?


This is the most striking lifecycle of any asset. When a GC gap survives Monday, it has a 72.7% chance of never filling that week. Gold gaps that don't fill Monday tend to be structural — if it doesn't close by NY PM, it probably won't close all week. This is the opposite of NQ where late-week fills (J+3, J+4) are substantial.

### Context

Gold's NWOG behaviour is fundamentally different from equities. The fast early fills (33% in 30 min, 56% in Asia) and the near-zero late-week fill rate make it a "fill early or not at all" asset. The low flip rate (48%) means fills tend to stop at the Friday close rather than reverse — trend continuation is the dominant mode.
